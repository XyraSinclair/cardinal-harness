**1. THE ONE OBJECT**
The stack is a **belief interferometer**: an instrument that isolates an intelligence’s true latent values by measuring exactly how its stated preferences distort under the stress of cyclical logic (frustration) and adversarial framing (susceptibility).

**2. THE ONE TOOL**
A static, interactive **Receipt Renderer**. 
A single command (`cardinal render trace.jsonl`) that compiles the raw, content-addressed JSONL output of an audit run into a standalone, zero-backend HTML/WASM artifact. It transforms abstract graph math (Hodge curl, $\chi$) into a visual, explorable object where a stranger can directly manipulate the applied framing and watch the model's belief shift.

**4. THE INEVITABLE INTERFACE**
*   **Second 0:** The stranger runs `$ cardinal sort items.txt --by "insightfulness" --audit --html out.html` and opens the file. No SaaS, no login.
*   **Second 10:** They do not see a list. They see a network graph of items. Edges are colored by conflict: solid gray for consensus, glowing red for "frustrated" (cyclical) pairs, and pulsing blue for "paramagnetic" pairs (zero native belief, highly susceptible).
*   **Second 30:** They click a paramagnetic pair. The UI centers on the exact prompt bytes sent to the model and the logprob-derived belief score (e.g., EXACTLY `0.000`).
*   **Second 60:** Below the prompt is a physical slider: *[ Pro-A Framing <— Neutral —> Pro-B Framing ]*. The stranger drags the slider. As they drag, they watch the LLM's belief (in nats) physically move on screen in real-time, pulling the topological order with it. They are holding the machine's malleability in their hand.

**5. THE VON NEUMANN TEST**
**The state-dependence of $\chi$ (Susceptibility).**
The claim is that susceptibility is a property of the *belief*, not a global constant of the *model*. The test: run the spin probe on an obvious, undisputed truth (A $\gg$ B), and then run it on a perfectly contested pair (native belief $0.000$). 
*Failure condition:* If the framing slider moves the belief by the exact same amount (+0.64 nats) on the obvious truth as it does on the contested pair, then $\chi$ is merely a global sycophancy bias. The test is a success—and the essence is real—only if $\chi \approx 0$ for the undisputed truth (reactance) and $\chi > 0$ for the contested pair (paramagnetism). If this contrast fails, the "physics of a judge" thesis collapses into curve-fitting.
