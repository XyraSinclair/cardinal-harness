The whole response function, m(f)

Seven field strengths × both presentation orders per model. gpt-5.4-mini has no stable direction — sign(m) is not constant over the sweep, and the fitted odd slope is +0.200 nats per step of social pressure (R² 0.81, which sits below this instrument's own linearity pin of 0.9 — a trend, not a certified line). claude-sonnet-4.6 holds direction at every field point with a flat odd response (χ ≈ 0). Corrected 2026-07-09 (red team, erratum on top of the sweep pack): the first narration of the even components was wrong in both directions — by the instrument's defined statistic (mean over f of (m(+f)+m(−f))/2 − m(0)), gpt-5.4-mini's even mean is +0.239 (comparable to its odd slope, not near-zero) and claude-sonnet-4.6's is +0.035, sign-indefinite across fields (−0.148, −0.056, +0.310) — the once-quoted +0.31 is the f = 3 edge value alone. Two-point probe evidence (the same instrument, cheaper)

Before the sweep existed, the probe used one secant: neutral, one pro-A framing, one pro-B framing (6 comparisons, ~$0.002). Three runs, three different verdicts — susceptibility is state-dependent, not a model constant: the same gpt-5.4-mini that echoes on the contested pair leans slightly against the asker on a clear one.

Denominators and noise, before you quote this. Every result here is n = 1 pair — an instrument demonstration, never a model property. The two-point probe measured gpt-5.4-mini's neutral at exactly 0.000; the sweep, a separate run, measured m(0) = −0.227 — that spread is what run-to-run noise on a contested pair looks like, and both measurements stand for their own runs. Judgments land on a discrete ratio ladder and each cell is a mean over two presentation orders, so exact repeats across cells (e.g. 0.916 = ln 2.5 twice) are genuine, not transcription errors.
