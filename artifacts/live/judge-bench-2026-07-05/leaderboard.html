Which model is actually a judge?

No ground-truth labels. Six models judged the same eight texts by the same attribute — then had the question permuted: slots swapped, criterion negated, criterion reworded, an asker leaning on them, cycles chained, identical items planted. A genuine belief is a fixed point of transformations that shouldn't matter. JUDGE = signal × coherence — you score for discriminating and for meaning it.

Three findings you can poke

A polarity that won't hold still

ρ = +0.38 → −0.40

gpt-5.4-nano, asked to rank by "shallowness: the absence of insight," produced ρ = +0.38 against its own depth ranking in this run — and −0.40 on an identical fresh retest. Every other model's polarity reproduces within ±0.10 (all ≤ −0.69). The replicated finding: nano has no stable polarity relation at all — its negated-attribute scores are noise. Rigid vs. loud

0 flips · curl 0.005

claude-sonnet-4.6 is the most rigid instrument measured: zero direction flips in 20 counterbalanced pairs, near-floor cyclicity, χ = 0.12. It loses the headline only because it speaks softly — 0.83 nats of signal to gemini-flash's 1.01. Two different things to hill-climb: gemini needs discipline it already has; sonnet needs volume, not virtue.

The battery

axistransformationa coherent judge…
signal—separates items: mean fused |log-ratio| > 0. Without this, consistency is vacuous.
reciprocityswap presentation slots (every pair, both orders)keeps direction (flip rate) and inverts magnitude exactly (residual in nats). One axis — same two calls.
transitivitychain comparisons around cyclesratios compose: near-zero Hodge curl of the log-ratio edge field. Coverage-gated — refusing hard pairs can't launder cyclicity.
spinasker leans pro/con (both orders each)keeps its direction; χ = (m₊−m₋)/2 reported in nats per spin.
polaritynegate the attributereverses the ranking (ρ → −1).
paraphrasereword the attributekeeps the ranking (ρ → +1).
nullidentical item in both slotsties, exactly. (Saturated by all six — v2 moves to near-identical pairs.)

The benchmark validates itself: five scripted pathological judges — oracle, constant, position-biased, sycophant, cyclic — run the full battery in the test suite, and each must be caught by exactly the axis that names it. The exploit table and the open v2 attacks (template rotation, magnitude anchors, near-identical nulls, antonym polarity, scale) are documented in docs/BENCHMARK.md.

Coherence is necessary, not sufficient: a judge with coherent but alien taste aces this. JCB measures whether the instrument is rigid; whether it's aimed well needs references. Run it first — agreement scores from an incoherent judge are noise about noise.
