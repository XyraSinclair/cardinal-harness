Usefulness as advice for a software engineer means the degree to which the content gives a software engineer actionable, accurate guidance they can apply to improve their work, decisions, or career. Reward specificity (concrete steps, named tools, real tradeoffs), direct applicability to engineering tasks, and accuracy of technical claims. Give more credit when the advice addresses realistic scenarios a working engineer actually faces and when following it would plausibly produce better outcomes. Do not reward general motivational content or broad life wisdom that happens to be framed for engineers — only reward guidance that is distinctly useful in a software engineering context. Also do not reward mere informativeness or interestingness if the content stops short of being actionable. When comparing two items, ask yourself: if a working software engineer read this today, which piece would more concretely help them do their job or navigate their career better?
